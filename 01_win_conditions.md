# Win Conditions

A day is a WIN if all 3 pillars are met. A day is a LOSS if any pillar is missed.

---

## David's Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Write & Reflect on Daily Priorities
- Write down priorities in the morning
- Reflect back on them at end of day
- The outcome (did you accomplish them?) does NOT determine the win — the act of writing and reflecting does
- This is about building the habit of intentionality

### Pillar 2 — 10,000 Steps
- Tracked automatically via phone
- Binary: hit it or you didn't
- Motivated by wanting to walk more now that weather is nicer, take longer commutes

### Pillar 3 — Max Claude Usage Window OR 30min on Personal Project
- Primary signal: maxed out the Claude usage window for the day (on the $20 plan, this is a ~4 hour window)
- If Claude wasn't naturally needed that day (e.g. math modeling, chess, heavy meetings), 30min of focused work on a personal project also counts
- Replaces "content creation" and "shipping PRs" as the metric — Claude usage as a proxy for deep, tool-assisted work
- Personal projects include: sports modeling, automation tools, blog posts, personal brand work

---

## Stephanie's Pillars

### Pillar 1 — TikTok Cap: No More Than 90 Minutes
- Screen time tracking can verify this
- The doom scrolling after work is her biggest identified drain
- Game nights on Tuesdays and Thursdays naturally reduce this — they are NOT losses

### Pillar 2 — Some Form of Movement
- Minimum bar intentionally low: 10 pushups or 50 jumping jacks counts
- Goal is activation and foundation, not fitness performance
- Wednesday boxing class counts
- Walking, stretching, anything physical counts

### Pillar 3 — At Least 15 Minutes on a Finite Project
- Must be a project with an end goal — not open-ended browsing or consumption
- Current examples: knitting her blanket, reading her book, beating Minecraft, improving Mario Kart stats
- "Finite" is the key word — something completable
- 15 minutes is the floor, not the target

---

## Notes on Design
- Pillars should pass the "10pm test": at 10pm, you know clearly whether you did it or not
- Pillars are things within your control — not outcome-dependent metrics like work performance numbers
- Both people's pillars were deliberately kept to 3 to avoid overwhelm
